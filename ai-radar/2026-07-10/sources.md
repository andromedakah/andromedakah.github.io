- Channel Dive, "AI will redirect $234B in enterprise software spending: Gartner" — https://www.channeldive.com/news/software-agentic-arbitrage-saaspocalypse-gartner/824309/
- Business Standard, "Agentic AI may put $234 bn of SaaS spending at risk by 2030: Gartner" — https://www.business-standard.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/gartner-agentic-ai-forecast-agentic-ai-enterprise-saas-spending-gartner-software-risk-126070100820_1.html
- Automation Today, "Report: Agentic Automation Could Put $234 Billion in SaaS Spending at Risk" — https://automationtoday.net/featuredarticles/report-agentic-automation-could-put-234-billion-in-saas-spending-at-risk/

Claims sourced here: up to $234B of enterprise application spend is exposed to "agentic arbitrage" between now and 2030, roughly 20% of enterprise-application SaaS spend by 2030; agentic arbitrage occurs when AI agents complete tasks across multiple systems, reducing direct interaction with UX-heavy applications and making the software "invisible," breaking the link between user growth and revenue growth; Gartner's guidance — incumbents should shift from interface-based to outcome-based value, embed agentic capabilities in business processes and preserve customer-specific knowledge, while AI-native startups and service providers can benefit by becoming the orchestration layer that coordinates work across enterprise applications.

- Deloitte, "SaaS meets AI agents: Transforming budgets, customer experience, and workforce dynamics" — https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-and-telecom-predictions/2026/saas-ai-agents.html

- SoftwareSeni, "SaaS Pricing Is Shifting from Per-Seat to Usage and Outcome" — https://www.softwareseni.com/saas-pricing-is-shifting-from-per-seat-to-usage-and-outcome-what-changes-at-your-next-renewal/
- Outlook (xhub), "SaaSpocalypse 2026: Agentic AI & The End Of Per-Seat SaaS" — https://www.outlookindia.com/xhub/blockchain-insights/the-saaspocalypse-of-2026-how-agentic-ai-killed-per-seat-saas
- Getmonetizely, "The 2026 Guide to SaaS, AI, and Agentic Pricing Models" — https://www.getmonetizely.com/blogs/the-2026-guide-to-saas-ai-and-agentic-pricing-models

Claims sourced here (as reported): Gartner projects at least 40% of enterprise SaaS spend will shift toward usage-, agent-, or outcome-based pricing by 2030; the early-2026 "SaaSpocalypse" wiped roughly $285B from software stock valuations; ~83% of AI-native SaaS companies already offer usage-based pricing (Maxio, via coverage); when one AI-agent-equipped worker can do the work of several employees, per-seat pricing that underpinned SaaS economics for two decades begins to break, with customers reducing seats rather than adding them. Quote — "The squeeze is on raw model APIs and on generic SaaS sitting in the middle." — Shyam Sankar, CTO, Palantir (a16z American Dynamism, April 2026, as reported).
