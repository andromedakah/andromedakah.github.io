The Operating Model Eats the Model
Saturday, 27 June 2026 · Cross-sector · C-level + Engineering
1 · Executive Summary

Enterprise AI shifted from “which model” to “which operating model.” The story is now agents that act, the plumbing that governs them, and the proprietary data that differentiates them.

1. Agents crossed the threshold. Gartner: 40% of apps embed agents by EOY 2026 (from <5%) — yet only 17% deployed, and >40% of projects cancelled by 2027.
2. “Token capital.” Nadella: value comes from proprietary models, self-hosted agents and secure pipelines — not rented APIs.
3. Cost/sovereignty pendulum. Microsoft shipped MAI-Code-1-Flash to cut reliance on OpenAI — a tell for your multi-vendor strategy.
4. Governance has a deadline. EU AI Act fully applicable 2 Aug 2026 (€35M / 7% turnover). 55% of staff use shadow AI; 37% of firms have a policy.
5. MCP arrived under the hood. The “USB-C of AI tools,” now connective tissue across major agent frameworks.

Bottom line: winners build governed, data-differentiated agent systems on top of what they already own.

2 · Allegory of the Day
🎭 “The Reservoir and the Grid”

Two cities got electricity the same year. City A plugged into the grid and paid by the kilowatt — modern overnight, but the operator set the rules and it never owned any power. City B also tapped the grid, but built a reservoir behind its own dam. When prices spiked it sold back; when the grid faltered its hospitals stayed lit. The reservoir became its real wealth.

Moral: renting frontier models buys parity (the grid); your proprietary data and owned workflows are the reservoir — token capital rivals can’t rent. The question: what fraction of our budget builds the reservoir vs. pays the meter?

4 · Technical — The Agentic Stack on Legacy

Agents don’t replace legacy — they sit on top, in four layers:

① Think — Reasoning models (GPT-5.5 · Gemini 3.x · Claude Opus)
② Know — Context engineering (memory + RAG + state)
③ Do — MCP action layer (governed read & write · tools)
④ Records — Legacy systems (ERP · CRM · Mainframe · Data lake)

RAG = knowing, MCP = doing, and they coexist. With MCP a legacy system is wrapped once and reused by any agent — write-once, reuse-many. The hard part isn’t the model; it’s legacy accessibility & data quality.
