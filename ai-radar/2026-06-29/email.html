1 · Executive Summary

This week's frontier headline was not a bigger general model. Value is migrating away from raw scale toward the specialized substrate underneath — your structured data, your power, your access to compute.

1. The frontier shipped behind a gate. OpenAI's GPT-5.6 (Sol/Terra/Luna) previewed 26 Jun — and the US government requested a restricted preview, which OpenAI granted ("not the norm"). Sol holds $5/$30 per 1M tok; new cache: 90%-off reads, 30-min lifetime.
2. SAP bet €1B+ that LLMs are wrong for business data. Buying Prior Labs (TabPFN) — Tabular Foundation Models built for the rows-and-columns that run companies, not LLMs with "rudimentary understanding of numbers."
3. The ceiling is energy, not algorithms. 4 clouds: >$710B AI capex for 2026; US data-center demand 74 GW by 2028 vs a ~49 GW shortfall. Huang: AI needs "~1,000× more energy."

Bottom line: the edge shifts from who rents the biggest model to who owns the right substrate — your data, your power, a multi-vendor access plan.

2 · Allegory of the Day
🎭 "The Poet and the Actuary"

A firm hired a dazzling poet (the LLM) — charming, eloquent on any subject. Then they handed him the ledger — claims, prices, churn — and asked what happens next quarter. He answered fluently… and wrong; he'd only ever read about tables. Down the hall, a quiet actuary (the tabular model) read the ledger cold, weighed the tails, priced the risk — and was right, again and again.

Moral: for the data that runs your business, you don't need a bigger poet — you need a better statistician. SAP just paid €1B+ to hire the actuary. The question: am I paying poet prices for actuarial work?

4 · Technical — Tabular Foundation Models vs LLMs

Two ways to predict from a table:

LLM path — table → text → tokens → eloquent guess. Costly, weak on numbers & tails.
TFM path (TabPFN) — rows in-context → one forward pass → calibrated prediction, no per-task training.

Your most-owned data already lives in tables. TFMs turn "a bespoke ML project per use case" into write-once, predict-many. The bottleneck stays data accessibility & quality, not the model. Full stack: agents do, RAG knows text, TFMs predict over tables.
