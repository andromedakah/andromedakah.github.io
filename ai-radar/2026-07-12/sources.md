## Apple v. OpenAI — trade-secret lawsuit (filed 10 July 2026, N.D. Cal.)

- TechCrunch, "Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade secret theft" — https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/
- NBC News, "Apple sues OpenAI and two former employees, accusing them of trade secrets theft" — https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/apple-sues-openai-two-former-employees-trade-secrets-theft-rcna385916
- Fortune, "Apple accuses OpenAI, and former design star Jony Ive's io Products firm, of stealing hardware trade secrets in blockbuster lawsuit" — https://fortune.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets-theft-allegations/
- CNBC, "Apple sues OpenAI alleging trade secret theft, says scheme was 'at every level'" — https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html

Claims sourced here (as reported, and as allegations in a pending complaint): Apple filed suit in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California alleging trade-secret theft and breach of contract against OpenAI (OpenAI Foundation and OpenAI Group PBC), io Products (Jony Ive's hardware venture), and two former Apple employees — Tang Yew Tan (former VP of product design for iPhone and Apple Watch) and Chang Liu (former senior systems electrical engineer, ~8 years at Apple). The complaint alleges a "coordinated" effort to obtain Apple's confidential product designs, manufacturing processes and supply-chain strategies, operating "at every level," including: Tan's alleged use of Apple's confidential project code names during OpenAI recruiting and requests that candidates bring Apple hardware components to interviews and coaching of departing employees on evading Apple's security procedures; and Chang Liu's alleged failure to return his Apple-issued laptop, which he allegedly used to download confidential Apple technical documents. Apple states that more than 400 former Apple employees now work at OpenAI. OpenAI's statement in response: "We have no interest in other companies' trade secrets. We remain focused on building innovative technology that empowers people everywhere."

## Insider IP risk — departing employees

- SC Media, "The rise of employee IP theft — and what to do about it" — https://www.scworld.com/perspective/the-rise-of-employee-ip-theft-and-what-to-do-about-it
- Breachsense, "How to Prevent Data Theft by Employees: 8 Strategies" — https://www.breachsense.com/blog/prevent-data-theft-employees/
- Jackson Lewis / Workplace Privacy, Data Management & Security Report, "Insider Threats: The Overlooked Risks of Departing Employees and Sensitive Data Theft" — https://www.workplaceprivacyreport.com/2025/01/articles/data-breach-notification/insider-threats-the-overlooked-risks-of-departing-employees-and-sensitive-data-theft/
- DeepStrike, "Insider Threat Statistics 2025: Key Data & Defense Strategies" — https://deepstrike.io/blog/insider-threat-statistics-2025
- Verizon, "2025 Data Breach Investigations Report (DBIR)" — https://www.verizon.com/business/resources/reports/dbir/

Claims sourced here (as reported): ~59% of departing employees take confidential company data with them (attributed to Ponemon Institute research); ~70% of insider intellectual-property theft occurs within 30 days of a resignation announcement; ~37% of organizations report IP loss when an employee departs (and ~61% have experienced insider-related data loss); internal actors are involved in ~30% of data breaches globally per the Verizon 2025 DBIR.

## GenAI as a data-exfiltration channel

- LayerX, "Enterprise AI and SaaS Data Security Report 2025" (via coverage) — https://www.esecurityplanet.com/news/shadow-ai-chatgpt-dlp/
- The Register, "Employees regularly paste company secrets into ChatGPT" — https://www.theregister.com/2025/10/07/gen_ai_shadow_it_secrets
- Cybersecurity Insiders, "Research says over 77 percent of data is shared to ChatGPT by employees" — https://www.cybersecurity-insiders.com/research-says-over-77-percent-of-data-is-shared-to-chatgpt-by-employees/
- Cyberhaven, "11% of data employees paste into ChatGPT is confidential" — https://www.cyberhaven.com/blog/4-2-of-workers-have-pasted-company-data-into-chatgpt

Claims sourced here (as reported): ~77% of employees paste data into GenAI tools; ~26.4% of file uploads to GenAI tools contain sensitive data (up from ~22% three months earlier); GenAI has become the number-one channel for corporate data exfiltration, accounting for ~32% of all unauthorized data movement; a majority of the technical data pasted into GenAI tools is proprietary source code (LayerX Enterprise AI & SaaS Data Security Report 2025). Cyberhaven separately reports that ~11% of the data employees paste into ChatGPT is confidential.

## EU AI Act — GPAI enforcement

- European Commission, "AI Act | Shaping Europe's digital future" — https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act, "Implementation Timeline" — https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/implementation-timeline/

Claims sourced here: the Commission's supervision and enforcement powers over GPAI providers become fully applicable on 2 August 2026; high-risk systems must meet logging, provenance/documentation and human-oversight requirements; the maximum GPAI penalty is €15M or 3% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.
