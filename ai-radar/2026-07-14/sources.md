## EU AI Act — GPAI enforcement (2 August 2026)

- European Commission, "AI Act | Shaping Europe's digital future" — https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act, "Implementation Timeline" — https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/implementation-timeline/
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act, "Enforcement of Chapter V under the EU AI Act" — https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/enforcement-of-chapter-v-under-the-eu-ai-act/
- Space Daily, "Europe's AI Act grace period ends and the Commission gains power to fine model makers" — https://spacedaily.com/m-europe-ai-act-commission-fines-august-2026/
- beam.ai, "EU AI Act 2026: GPAI Enforcement & 3% Fines Begin" — https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/eu-ai-act-enforcement-august-2-2026-gpai-fines
- axis-intelligence, "EU AI Act News 2026: The Revised Timeline, August Enforcement & Omnibus Deal Explained" — https://axis-intelligence.com/eu-ai-act-news/
- Tech Times, "EU AI Act Enforcement Is Here: Chatbot Rules Live, High-Risk AI Delay Now Binding Law" — https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320101/20260710/eu-ai-act-enforcement-here-chatbot-rules-live-high-risk-ai-delay-now-binding-law.htm

Claims sourced here (as reported): On 2 August 2026 the enforcement and penalty powers of the EU AI Act over general-purpose AI (GPAI) providers become fully applicable. The GPAI obligations and the Code of Practice have been in force since August 2025; the supervision and enforcement machinery was held back for roughly a year to let providers and the AI Office operationalize, and that grace period ends on 2 August 2026. From that date the European Commission and its AI Office can request information and documentation, run technical evaluations of a model, require compliance and risk-mitigation measures, restrict/recall/withdraw a model from the EU market, and impose fines of up to €15M or 3% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher (Article 101).

## EU AI Act — Digital Omnibus (high-risk deadline changes)

- Gibson Dunn, "EU AI Act Omnibus Agreement — Postponed High-Risk Deadlines and Other Key Changes" — https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/
- Inside Global Tech (Covington), "EU AI Act Update: Timeline Relief, Targeted Simplification, and New Prohibitions" — https://www.insideglobaltech.com/2026/05/28/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions/
- DataGuidance, "EU: Commission confirms absence of grace period for AI Act implementation" — https://www.dataguidance.com/news/eu-commission-confirms-absence-grace-period-ai-act

Claims sourced here (as reported): In May 2026 EU negotiators reached a provisional agreement on the "Digital Omnibus," which pushes back several high-risk (Annex III) deadlines. The relief reshuffles the high-risk timeline but does not touch the 2 August 2026 GPAI enforcement date, which remains standing.

## GPAI Code of Practice — signatories and Meta's refusal

- General-Purpose AI Code of Practice — Wikipedia — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General-Purpose_AI_Code_of_Practice
- TechCrunch, "Meta refuses to sign EU's AI code of practice" — https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/18/meta-refuses-to-sign-eus-ai-code-of-practice/
- AI Act Blog, "Who (and why) the biggest AI players embrace — or reject — the new EU 'Code of Practice'" — https://www.aiactblog.nl/en/posts/eu-code-practice-company-reactions
- Jones Day, "EU AI Act: European Commission Publishes General-Purpose AI Code of Practice" — https://www.jonesday.com/en/insights/2025/08/eu-ai-act-european-commission-publishes-generalpurpose-ai-code-of-practice

Claims sourced here (as reported): The GPAI Code of Practice was published 10 July 2025 and endorsed by the Commission in August 2025; roughly 26 organizations signed, including Amazon, Anthropic, Google, IBM, Microsoft, OpenAI, Mistral AI, Cohere and Aleph Alpha. Meta publicly declined to sign, citing "legal uncertainties" and arguing the Code "goes beyond the AI Act," particularly on dataset documentation and copyright obligations.

## Enterprise EU AI Act readiness gap

- Vision Compliance, "2026 EU AI Act Readiness Report" (via EIN Presswire) — https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/903074846/vision-compliance-releases-2026-eu-ai-act-readiness-report-finds-78-of-enterprises-unprepared-for-obligations
- National Law Review, "Vision Compliance Releases 2026 EU AI Act Readiness Report, Finds 78% of Enterprises Unprepared" — https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/vision-compliance-releases-2026-eu-ai-act-readiness-report-finds-78
- Kiteworks, "EU AI Act August 2, 2026 Deadline Is Six Weeks Away. Most Organizations Are Not Ready." — https://www.kiteworks.com/regulatory-compliance/eu-ai-act-deadline-compliance/

Claims sourced here (as reported): Per the Vision Compliance 2026 EU AI Act Readiness Report, 78% of organizations have taken no meaningful steps toward compliance, 74% lack a designated internal owner or governance body for AI compliance, 61% have no process for generating the technical documentation required for high-risk AI systems, and 83% have no formal AI system inventory. Related coverage cites ~40% of enterprise AI systems with unclear risk classification (appliedAI study of 106 enterprise systems).

## Agent adoption, governance maturity & agent security

- Gartner, "Gartner Predicts 40% of Enterprise Apps Will Feature Task-Specific AI Agents by 2026, Up From Less Than 5% in 2025" (26 Aug 2025) — https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025
- Agentic AI Institute, "Agentic AI Enterprise Adoption 2026: 72% Production Proven" — https://agenticaiinstitute.org/agentic-ai-enterprise-adoption-2026-governance-gap/
- Gravitee, "State of AI Agent Security 2026 Report: When Adoption Outpaces Control" — https://www.gravitee.io/blog/state-of-ai-agent-security-2026-report-when-adoption-outpaces-control

Claims sourced here (as reported): Gartner projects 40% of enterprise applications will feature task-specific AI agents by end-2026 (up from less than 5% in 2025) and that more than 40% of agentic-AI projects will be cancelled by end-2027 due to governance and ROI failures. Only about 21% of organizations have a mature governance model for AI agents. Per Gravitee's State of AI Agent Security 2026, 88% of organizations reported a confirmed or suspected AI agent security incident in the past year.
