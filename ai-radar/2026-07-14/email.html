The Learner's Permit

Tuesday, 14 July 2026 · Cross-sector edition · C-level + Engineering

The governance thesis gets teeth from the law, with a clock attached. In 19 days — on 2 August 2026 — the EU's year-long grace period on general-purpose AI ends, and the Commission's AI Office can demand documentation, run its own evaluations, order risk mitigations, fine providers up to €15M or 3% of global turnover, and pull a model off the EU market. The rules were posted in August 2025; only now does the examiner get a ticket book and a tow truck. And the drivers aren't ready: 78% of organizations have taken no meaningful compliance steps and 83% have no AI inventory. A learner's permit doesn't renew.

1 · Executive Summary

On 2 August 2026, the enforcement and penalty machinery of the EU AI Act for general-purpose AI (GPAI) becomes fully applicable. The obligations and Code of Practice have been "live" since August 2025, but the Commission held its teeth back a year. That grace period ends in 19 days. From then the AI Office can request documentation, run technical evaluations, demand risk mitigations, restrict or withdraw a model from the EU market, and fine up to €15M or 3% of global turnover (Article 101). The May 2026 Digital Omnibus pushed back high-risk deadlines but left the 2 August GPAI date standing.

1. The deadline is real, dated, and reaches deployers. A specific date, specific powers (evaluate, mitigate, restrict, withdraw, fine), a specific ceiling. Deploy a GPAI model or an agent built on one and you inherit inventory, risk-classification, logging, documentation and human-oversight duties — now required, not merely wise.

2. Almost nobody is ready. 78% of organizations have taken no meaningful steps, 83% have no AI inventory, 74% have no compliance owner, 61% can't produce the technical documentation (Vision Compliance 2026). Only 21% have a mature agent-governance model — even as 40% of enterprise apps embed agents by end-2026 and 88% reported an agent incident.

3. Use the last of the grace period. Don't panic-buy a tool on 1 August — do this week what the prepared did all year: draw the AI inventory, classify risk, name an owner, stand up logging-and-oversight evidence. Meanwhile 56% of CEOs report no measurable AI benefit yet (PwC 2026): the governance bill is due before the value.

Bottom line: the rules were posted a year ago; on 2 August the examiner gets a badge, a ticket book and a tow truck. A learner's permit doesn't renew. Can you produce an inventory, an owner and an audit trail before it does?

2 · Allegory — The Learner's Permit

When a city opens a new highway, it posts the code and issues a learner's permit before switching on the cameras — a grace period for drivers to learn the road while the police carry no ticket book. The careful use it to practice; the rest see the silent cameras as proof nothing changed.

The EU opened that highway in August 2025. On 2 August 2026 the permit expires: the AI Office gets its ticket book — documentation demands, its own evaluations, risk-mitigation orders, fines up to €15M or 3% of turnover — and the tow truck: power to restrict or withdraw a model from the EU market. The road is the same; the cameras are now wired to consequences. And 78% of drivers never booked a lesson — no inventory (83%), no owner (74%), no paperwork (61%).

The question it forces: The permit expires in 19 days. Can we produce, this week, an inventory of every AI system we run, its risk class, its provider's compliance status, and the person accountable for it — or are we still assuming the cameras stay dark?

3 · Technical — When the Grace Period Ends

The obligations existed since August 2025; what changes on 2 August is that the AI Office can act — request documentation, run evaluations, order mitigations, restrict/withdraw a model, and fine up to €15M or 3% of turnover. And it reaches deployers, not just labs: obligations flow down the chain. A stricter deadline isn't coming — the Digital Omnibus moved high-risk dates and left GPAI standing. The fix isn't a purchase; it's a week of inventory, classification and ownership.

AI is often the least-documented layer on legacy estates; the retrofit — inventory, risk-classify, assign an owner, wire logging and human oversight into the systems of record — is achievable in three weeks, and the compliance evidence is the good-governance evidence.

5 · So What — 3 Moves for the Next 30 Days

1. Draw the AI inventory this week — the thing 83% don't have. List every AI/GPAI system and agent (shadow ones included) and record the base model, the provider, whether that provider signed the Code of Practice, and a first-pass risk class. It needs no budget approval and every other control depends on it.

2. Name an owner and stand up the evidence. Appoint a single, board-adjacent, accountable owner (74% have none) and start maintaining the technical documentation, logs and human-oversight records the Act requires (61% can't). On 2 August this control plane stops being good practice and becomes your compliance evidence.

3. Check the model supply chain and pre-wire the fallback. Confirm each GPAI provider's compliance and Code-of-Practice status; for any model that could face restriction or withdrawal, have a model-neutral fallback ready (the 7 Jul router). Govern for the outcome and the audit trail — the examiner gets a ticket book in 19 days.
