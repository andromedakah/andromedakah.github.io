2 · Allegory — The Aluminum Age

For most of the 19th century, aluminum was one of the most precious metals on Earth — despite being the most abundant metal in the crust. The scarcity was never the element; it was the unlocking. Napoleon III is said to have reserved aluminum cutlery for his most honored guests; the Washington Monument (1884) was capped with a ~6-pound aluminum apex, briefly displayed at Tiffany's like a jewel. Then in 1886 Hall and Héroult cracked cheap electrolytic smelting, and the price fell ~99% in a decade — from dearer than silver to foil you'd wrap a sandwich in. Everyone holding ingots watched their treasure turn to tin. Kimi K3 is the Hall-Héroult process for frontier intelligence: a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model any firm or nation can soon smelt on its own hardware, at a third of yesterday's token price.

The part the board must sit with: the value didn't disappear when the price collapsed — it migrated. Off the ingot and into what people built with the cheap metal — aerospace, packaging, electrification. The airframe, not the bar. Your moat was never the model. It's the data no open weight can see, the workflow and distribution it can't own, the governance the regulator makes you prove, and a plant flexible enough to run tomorrow's commodity in place of today's.

The question it forces: If a frontier-class model is now a commodity metal — open-weight, self-hostable, priced like a mid-tier tool — is our moat still the ingot we license, which anyone can now smelt or download, or the airframe we build with it: the data, workflow, distribution and governance the cheap metal can never copy?

1. Re-price your stack at commodity metal — and add the open model as a governed fallback. Frontier-class capability now costs ~$3 per million tokens and is free to self-host on 27 July. Re-run the unit economics of every AI feature at commodity input cost, and — via your model-neutral router (7 Jul) — qualify an open-weight model as a sovereign fallback for cost, continuity and data-residency.

2. Name your airframe and invest there, not in the ingot. Value is shifting to those who distribute and deploy.
