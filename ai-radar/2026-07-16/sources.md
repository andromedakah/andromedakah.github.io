Claims sourced here (as reported): On 14 July 2026 Demis Hassabis (co-founder and CEO of Google DeepMind, Nobel laureate) published a ~10,000-word personal manifesto, "A Framework for Frontier AI and the Dawning of a New Age," proposing a US-led, FINRA-style, industry-funded Frontier AI Standards Body to test frontier models before deployment. Frontier labs would initially share models voluntarily, up to 30 days before release, for safety testing probing dangerous cyber, biological and "deception" capabilities (also guardrail-bypass and AI-image watermarking). The proposed board would be majority-independent (Turing Award winners and credentialed technical experts alongside industry, government and open-source representatives). Once the regime proved "effective and robust," passing it would become a prerequisite to deploy in the US market; the body could be "ratcheted up" as risks grow, including "coordinating a slowdown in development among the Frontier Labs if deemed necessary." The framework would cover every frontier-class model — domestic or foreign, proprietary or open-weight — with thresholds updated as capability grows. Hassabis wants the body operational before year-end.

## Context, rival proposals and critique

- Qz / TechCrunch (above) — Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei has instead called for a distinct, FAA-style agency with the power to block unsafe models outright.
- Brownstone Research, "The AI Standards Body That Shouldn't Exist" — https://www.brownstoneresearch.com/bleeding-edge/the-ai-standards-body-that-shouldnt-exist/
- Chat GPT Is Eating the World, "Demis Hassabis calls for testing of AI models by Standards Body modeled on FINRA" — https://chatgptiseatingtheworld.com/2026/07/15/demis-hassabis-calls-for-testing-of-ai-models-by-standards-body-modeled-on-finra-idea-sounds-similar-to-proposal-by-mark-thomas-at-lawfare/
- The Deep View, "Demis Hassabis outlines ambitious AI safety plan" — https://www.thedeepview.com/newsletter/demis-hassabis-outlines-ambitious-ai-safety-plan

Claims sourced here (as reported): Critics warn the proposal is a template for regulatory capture — a body funded and informed by the frontier labs that helps design the benchmarks it grades them against, with the risk of standards that favor incumbents and raise rivals' costs. Skeptics argue "we do not necessarily need a new bureaucracy standing between every model release and the market." The proposal comes roughly a month after Hassabis and Dario Amodei jointly urged G7 leaders at a closed-door session in Évian-les-Bains, France, to back a US-led AI governance framework, and it echoes a proposal floated at Lawfare.

## EU AI Act — GPAI enforcement (the mandatory counterweight)

- European Commission, "AI Act | Shaping Europe's digital future" — https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act, "Implementation Timeline" — https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/implementation-timeline/
- beam.ai, "EU AI Act 2026: GPAI Enforcement & 3% Fines Begin" — https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/eu-ai-act-enforcement-august-2-2026-gpai-fines
- ComplianceHub.Wiki, "60 Days to EU AI Act Enforcement" — https://compliancehub.wiki/eu-ai-act-august-2-2026-60-day-countdown-synthesis/

Claims sourced here: On 2 August 2026 the enforcement and penalty powers of the EU AI Act over general-purpose AI (GPAI) providers become fully applicable, including fines of up to €15M or 3% of global annual turnover (Article 101); the AI Office can request documentation, run technical evaluations, demand risk-mitigation measures, and restrict or withdraw a model from the EU market; obligations flow down to deployers. The May 2026 Digital Omnibus reshuffled high-risk deadlines but left the 2 August GPAI date standing. Industry surveys report ~78% of organizations have taken no meaningful steps toward compliance (as reported). The "17 days" figure is a simple count from this edition's date (16 July 2026) to 2 August 2026 and is the radar's own.
