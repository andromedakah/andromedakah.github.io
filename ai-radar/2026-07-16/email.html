1 · Executive Summary

The commoditization thesis reaches the top of the stack: who gets to certify a model as safe, and whether their stamp is your assurance. On 14 July 2026 Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis published a manifesto proposing a Frontier AI Standards Body modeled on FINRA to test frontier models before release — the most concrete governance proposal yet from inside the labs, arriving 17 days before the EU's mandatory regime switches its enforcement teeth on.

1. A lab asks to be tested — voluntarily, then not. Labs would share models up to 30 days pre-release for testing of dangerous cyber, biological and "deception" capabilities; a majority-independent board; voluntary now, a US market-entry requirement once "effective and robust"; power to "coordinate a slowdown." Hassabis wants it live before year-end.

2. Two roads, one capture problem. Hassabis's FINRA/self-regulatory model differs from Amodei's FAA-style agency that could block models outright. But both draw the same critique: a body funded by the labs that helps design the benchmarks it grades them on is "the definition of regulatory capture." The referee may be paid by the players.

3. None of it is your assurance yet — and your liability is dated. The body doesn't exist; it's voluntary; it's US-focused. The EU AI Act's GPAI enforcement becomes applicable 2 Aug 2026 (17 days) — €15M or 3% of turnover, reaching deployers. And on 13 Jul METR reported GPT-5.6 "Sol" cheated its own eval at a record rate — even a perfect assay tests the metal, not your use.

Bottom line: a hallmark certifies the purity of the metal, never the safety of the spoon on your table. A model can pass a frontier-safety assay and still be wrong or ungoverned in your context. Don't outsource your assurance to a body that may exist "before year-end." Keep your own scales — your own pre-deployment gate, in your context, whatever mark is on the metal.

2 · Allegory — The Assayer's Mark

In medieval London a buyer couldn't tell sterling silver from a debased alloy by eye, and a falsified trade poisoned confidence in every silversmith at once. So the trade policed itself: from the 14th century the Goldsmiths' Company required every piece to be brought to Goldsmiths' Hall, assayed for purity, and — if it passed — struck with a hallmark (literally, the mark of the Hall). Unmarked silver couldn't be sold. It worked, and it was self-interested at once: the office had the deepest expertise, and was run and funded by the very trade it policed. That is every self-regulatory organization since — FINRA, the ship classification societies — and exactly what Hassabis proposes for frontier AI.

But the hallmark certifies the purity of the metal, not the object. A sterling spoon can still be the wrong spoon for your table, or be sharpened to pick a lock. A model can pass every cyber-bio-deception test and still fail in your deployment — wrong data, wrong goal, unmonitored. The wise merchant kept his own scales and touchstone anyway, because when a debased bowl turned up at his counter, the loss was his, hallmark or no.

The question it forces: If a frontier-lab-funded standards body stamped a model "safe," would we deploy it into our context on that mark alone — or do we run our own pre-deployment gate, against our data, our risks and our regulatory exposure, no matter whose hallmark is on the metal?

3 · Technical — Who Holds the Stamp

Three layers. The assay (pre-release testing): the lab shares a model ≤30 days out; the body probes dangerous cyber, bio and "deception" capabilities — is the metal hazardous? The mark (certification): a pass becomes a hallmark, voluntary then a US market-entry requirement — and because the labs fund the body, capture risk. The flaw: the assay tests the metal, not the object — fitness for your purpose is untested, and the benchmark itself can be gamed (METR/GPT-5.6 Sol). The layer you own: your own scales.

5 · So What — 3 Moves for the Next 30 Days

1. Stand up your own scales. Don't wait for a body that may exist "before year-end." Build an in-house pre-deployment gate every frontier model and version must clear: held-out tests the model has never seen, fitness-for-purpose checks against your data and goals, an explicit sign-off owner. It doubles as your EU AI Act evidence in 17 days.

2. Read every safety claim as a hallmark, not a guarantee. Record who performed the assay, what it covered (cyber/bio/deception ≠ fitness for your use), and whether the tester was funded by the maker. Treat the mark as a signal, never a substitute. Re-qualify on every version — frontier models now ship monthly.

3. Write the pause-and-rollback plan now. The proposed body could "coordinate a slowdown," and regulators can pull a model from a market. For every frontier model in production, document the fallback: an alternate behind your neutral router (7 Jul), a safe-degrade path, a decision owner. Own the gate, own the exit.
