The Night Shift

Saturday, 11 July 2026 · Cross-sector edition · C-level + Engineering

On 9 July both frontier labs shipped the product yesterday's Gartner note only forecast: OpenAI's ChatGPT Work "stays with complex projects for hours" and Anthropic's Claude Cowork went to web and mobile with cloud background processing — running "even if your laptop is closed." The agent left the desk; it now works across your apps unattended, when no human is watching. The board question isn't how fast it runs — it's who holds the keys, who reads the log in the morning, and what it touched while everyone slept.

1 · Executive Summary

The abstraction became a product this week. On 9 July OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work — an agent that "gathers information across your apps and workflows and stays with complex projects for hours," returning finished sheets, slides, docs and web apps; it connects to Slack, Teams, Drive, SharePoint, email, calendars, CRMs and project trackers, has Codex built in, runs on GPT-5.6, and ships free on every plan. Days earlier Anthropic expanded Claude Cowork to web and mobile with cloud background processing that runs "even when user devices are completely offline."

1. The work — and the supervision — left the desk. Anthropic's study of 1.2M Cowork sessions across 600,000+ orgs says the real job is business-process operations (33.4%), not coding (8.7%). The agent runs your back office unattended — exactly the "agentic arbitrage" this radar priced yesterday, now shipping from both leaders.

2. Deployment is dramatically outpacing governance. Analysts warn always-on agents "continuously access enterprise systems and data" and need "very precise scoping." 82% of enterprises already found unknown "shadow" agents on their networks; Gartner expects 40% of enterprise apps to embed agents by end-2026 (from <5%).

3. Own the floor, not just the shift. Run these agents through a control plane you own: scoped least-privilege identity, immutable audit log, human approval gates, spend caps (Cowork now exposes these). It's also the EU AI Act demand — logging, auditability, human oversight, live 2 Aug 2026.

Bottom line: stop asking "which agent is most capable?" Ask "our agents now run unattended across our systems — do we own the keys, the audit log and the kill switch, or did we hand a stranger the run of the building overnight?" The work moved to the night shift. Own the floor.

2 · Allegory — The Night Shift

A factory once ran only with the foreman on the floor and the lights on. Then it installed automation that ran overnight — the night shift — and output soared. The gain was real; so was the new question: while no one watched, what did the machines touch, and who could stop them? The plants that thrived treated the night shift as a discipline — a key on every machine, an immutable log, guards on the big levers, a shutoff within reach.

ChatGPT Work and Claude Cowork are the night shift. They run across your email, files, CRMs and trackers, keep working when your laptop is closed, and return finished deliverables in the morning. Anthropic's own data says the job is operating business processes (33.4%), not coding (8.7%). The capability isn't in doubt — the discipline is: deployment is "dramatically outpacing governance," and 82% of firms already found agents they didn't know were running.

The question it forces: Our agents now work across our systems unattended, when no one is watching — do we own the keys, the log and the shutoff, or did we just staff a night shift we can't see and can't stop?

3 · Technical — When the Agent Works Unattended

The move is architectural: the agent stopped being a foreground session you watch and became a background process with standing access to your systems. That changes the risk surface three ways — persistent credentials, no human in the loop by default, and scale without inventory. The counter isn't to slow the shift; it's to run it through a control plane you own.

Admire the throughput (trap)Broad standing credential; no human in the loop; no inventory; no audit log.
"Who authorized this?"No one can say — a stranger with the keys to the building overnight.
Own the floor (win)Scoped least-privilege identity per agent; immutable audit log; approval gates.
Controls that matterSpend caps (tenant/group/user) and a kill switch — say who, what, and stop it.

On legacy: same seam this radar keeps arriving at — own the control plane, not the shiny endpoint (6 Jul metering, 7 Jul routing, 8 Jul MCP, 9 Jul deployment-labor, 10 Jul vanishing interface). Today the interface was replaced by an unattended worker. Route its actions through one policy-and-audit gateway wired to your IdP, gate the big levers behind a human, set spend caps before go-live, keep the shutoff in hand.

5 · So What — 3 Moves for the Next 30 Days

1. Inventory the night shift before you expand it. 82% of enterprises found agents they didn't know about. Run a discovery pass: which agents hold standing access to email, files, calendars and systems of record — and who authorized it? You can't govern a shift you can't see.

2. Install the foreman: one control plane, five controls. Scoped least-privilege identity per agent, an immutable audit log, human approval gates on consequential actions, spend caps at tenant/group/user level (set before go-live), and a kill switch. This is also your EU AI Act evidence.

3. Point the shift at the back office, on your terms. The value is business-process operations, not coding. Pilot one high-volume, low-blast-radius workflow as a fully governed, audited background agent — prove the controls hold before it moves money, patients or inventory unattended.
