- MarketScale, "Enterprise AI's center of gravity shifts from models to orchestration, governance, and ROI clarity" — https://www.marketscale.com/industries/software-and-technology/enterprise-ais-center-of-gravity-shifts-from-models-to-orchestration-governance-and-roi-clarity

Claims sourced here (as reported): current coverage frames enterprise AI's centre of gravity as shifting "from models to orchestration, governance and ROI clarity"; CFOs are moving to impose budget controls, replacing open-ended experimentation with a demand for measurable returns; Forrester finds enterprises are postponing roughly 25% of planned AI spend to 2027 as financial scrutiny increases; Deloitte finds only 4% of organizations currently report AI value to the board (expected to become standard for large enterprises by end-2026); and a Gartner survey found fewer than one-third of corporate decision-makers could identify specific financial outcomes attributable to their AI investments.

## The pilot-to-production gap

### MIT — the GenAI Divide (95% of pilots deliver no P&L)

- MIT Project NANDA, "The GenAI Divide: State of AI in Business 2025" (relayed via coverage) — https://www.healthcareitnews.com/news/mit-95-enterprise-ai-pilots-fail-deliver-measurable-roi
- Virtualization Review, "MIT Report Finds Most AI Business Investments Fail, Reveals 'GenAI Divide'" — https://virtualizationreview.com/articles/2025/08/19/mit-report-finds-most-ai-business-investments-fail-reveals-genai-divide.aspx
- Legal.io, "MIT Report Finds 95% of AI Pilots Fail to Deliver ROI, Exposing 'GenAI Divide'" — https://www.legal.io/blog/5719519/MIT-Report-Finds-95-of-AI-Pilots-Fail-to-Deliver-ROI-Exposing-GenAI-Divide
- Ellvero, "AI ROI in 2026: Why 95% of Pilots Fail to Deliver and How to Measure What Actually Matters" — https://www.ellvero.com/insights/ai-roi-in-2026-why-95-percent-of-pilots-fail-and-how-to-measure-what-matters

Claims sourced here (as reported): MIT's Project NANDA report "The GenAI Divide: State of AI in Business" finds that 95% of generative-AI pilots deliver no measurable P&L impact and only ~5% of integrated pilots extract significant value; the study is based on 52 executive interviews, surveys of 153 leaders and analysis of 300 public AI deployments; and its diagnosis is that pilots fail on production realities ("pilots lie — they run on clean data, isolated systems and goodwill; production introduces ownership, security, integration, edge cases and accountability"), not on model quality.

Claims sourced here: Gartner predicts more than 40% of agentic-AI projects will be canceled by end-2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value or inadequate risk controls; the Anushree Verma (Senior Director Analyst, Gartner) quote — "Most agentic AI projects right now are early stage experiments or proof of concepts that are mostly driven by hype and are often misapplied" — is from Gartner's June-2025 agentic-AI forecast. Gartner projects 40% of enterprise applications will feature task-specific AI agents by end-2026 (up from <5% in 2025); estimates only ~130 of thousands of vendors claiming agentic AI truly deliver it ("agent washing"). The figure that 89% of agent pilots never reach production and that the ~11% surviving deliver ~171% ROI is relayed from analyst/industry coverage (as reported).

- BCG, "AI at Scale 2026" survey (~1,800 executives, relayed via coverage) — https://www.terminal-x.ai/research/ai-roi-in-2026-why-most-enterprise-ai-fails-and-what-actually-works

- Paul Okhrem, "50+ Enterprise AI Agent Statistics (2026)" — https://paul-okhrem.com/enterprise-ai-agents-statistics-2026/

Claims sourced here (as reported): BCG's AI at Scale 2026 survey of ~1,800 executives found only 26% of companies have generated meaningful financial value from their AI investments; PwC's 29th Global CEO Survey (Davos, January 2026) found 56% of CEOs report AI produced neither increased revenue nor decreased costs over the prior twelve months, while only 12% achieved both; Bain's Agentic AI Benchmark 2026 reports median payback of ~4.1 months for customer service, ~6.7 months for marketing operations and ~9.3 months for engineering; Morgan Stanley (relayed via coverage) found only ~21% of S&P 500 companies could cite a measurable AI benefit and IBM put initiatives delivering expected ROI at ~25%.

## The mandatory counterweight — EU AI Act GPAI enforcement (applicable 2 August 2026)

- European Commission, "AI Act | Shaping Europe's digital future" — https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act, "Enforcement of Chapter V under the EU AI Act" — https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/enforcement-of-chapter-v-under-the-eu-ai-act/
- beam.ai, "EU AI Act 2026: GPAI Enforcement & 3% Fines Begin" — https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/eu-ai-act-enforcement-august-2-2026-gpai-fines

Claims sourced here: From 2 August 2026 the European Commission's enforcement powers over general-purpose AI (GPAI) providers become fully applicable — including the power to request documentation and information (Article 91), conduct evaluations (Article 92), request measures including risk mitigation and market restriction (Article 93), and impose fines of up to €15 million or 3% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher (Article 101). The May-2026 Digital Omnibus deferred certain high-risk (Annex III) obligations to December 2027 but left the 2 August 2026 GPAI enforcement date standing. Obligations reach deployers, not only model providers.
