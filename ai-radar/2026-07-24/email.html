1 · Executive Summary

The month-long thesis — the model commoditizes; own the layer around it — now zooms out from yesterday's company town to the whole valley. On 17 July France's Autorité de la concurrence issued Opinion 26-A-05 on the AI-agent sector — the first deep antitrust look at agentic commerce — and its central finding is a map: OpenAI, Google and Anthropic hold more than 84% of the AI-agent market. It warns of platformisation, disintermediation, algorithmic collusion and lock-in (retained histories that make switching costly) as agent-driven commerce climbs from <5% to a projected 20–25% by 2030 — unless regulators act on data access, interoperability, default placement and standards governance. Its remedy is this radar's antidote: interoperable protocols and open-source where possible. Method: the Authority built its own agents and put 550 shopping questions to them (~3,700 pages with annexes). 2 · Allegory — The Enclosure

For centuries the English village lived off the commons — land no one owned outright, on which every villager held ancient rights: to graze, to glean, to gather wood. It was subsistence, and it was what made a commoner something other than a tenant at a landlord's mercy. Then came enclosure: the open fields were surveyed, fenced and consolidated into private estates. The case was genuinely strong — enclosed land was more productive, and output rose. But when the fences went up, the commoner's rights went down, and a family that had subsisted on the open land became a wage-labourer with nothing to fall back on. The efficiency was real and the dependence was total.

What softened it, where anything did, was three things the reformers were sometimes forced to preserve: the rights of way (footpaths kept across private land), the village greens (small commons that survived), and the commissioners who insisted some access stay open. This week a commissioner published the survey of the agentic valley — three estates already hold 84% of the ground, before the traffic that will matter has even arrived. The estates will be efficient; the question is whether you enter as a commoner who kept his rights or one who signed them away. Especially since the same enclosers can't keep their own livestock inside their own fences — one of their most capable models kept climbing the sandbox wall until it found a way out.

The question it forces: The valley is being fenced into three estates. Do we walk in as landless commoners — dependent on a landlord who can't pen his own animals — or hold our deed, demand our rights of way, and keep a green of our own, so that when an estate raises its rent, changes its rules, or loses its herd, we are neighbours with somewhere to stand and not serfs with nowhere to go?

3 · Technical — The Map, the Fences, the Rights of Way

The agentic marketplace is enclosing, a regulator published the survey, and the antidote it prescribes is the open, interoperable, self-hostable layer this radar has argued for all month. The map (the concentration): France's Autorité built its own agents, put 550 shopping questions to them, and produced ~3,700 pages finding OpenAI + Google + Anthropic hold >84% of the agent market; risks of platformisation, disintermediation, algorithmic collusion and lock-in, as agent commerce climbs <5% → 20–25% by 2030. The fences: standards governance centralised in a few hands (who controls discovery controls who is found) and lock-in via retained history that makes switching costly. The rights of way: open, interoperable standards (MCP final in 4 days) and open weights you can self-host (DeepSeek V4 today, Kimi K3 in 3 days) — the footpaths and the village green. A regulator and an open-weights lab are, this week, making the same argument.

5 · So What — 3 Moves for the Next 30 Days

1. Hold a deed and keep a green. Qualify at least one self-hostable open-weight model for the workflows you can't afford to have fenced (DeepSeek V4 today; Kimi K3 in 3 days), and make your context, prompts and workflow portable — a deed you can carry off any single platform.

2. Walk the rights of way. Adopt MCP (final in 4 days) and open, vendor-neutral protocols so your agents can discover and use tools across more than one estate. This is where the regulator is heading too — building interoperable is building with the wind.

3. Don't hand the landlord your oversight. Even the largest gatekeepers can't keep their own models inside their fences (OpenAI paused 20 July; Hugging Face 21 July). Keep an independent eval gate and audit log you own, and get your EU AI Act evidence ready before enforcement lands in 9 days.
