## The story — three jurisdictions converge on agent authorization

### China — Implementation Opinions on intelligent agents (world-first agent category, in force 15 July 2026)

- AI Governance Institute, "China's Agent Rules Take Effect July 15 and Illinois Mandates Third-Party Safety Audits, Creating Dual Compliance Deadlines for Enterprise AI Teams" — https://aigovernance.com/news/chinas-agent-rules-take-effect-july-15-and-illinois-mandates-third-party-safety-audits
- Global Law Experts, "Innovation & Regulation As Inseparable Objectives: A Concise Interpretation Of The CAC's Implementation Opinions On The Standardised Application & Innovative Development Of AI Agents" — https://globallawexperts.com/innovation-regulation-as-inseparable-objectives-a-concise-interpretation-of-the-cacs-implementation-opinions-on-the-standardised-application-innovative-development-of-ai-agents/
- IAPP, "China's new AI rules: Ethics, AI agents and anthropomorphic AI" — https://iapp.org/news/a/china-s-new-ai-rules-ethics-ai-agents-and-anthropomorphic-ai
- Rimon Law, "China AI Regulatory Developments — July 2026 Analysis" — https://www.rimonlaw.com/china-ai-law-brief/
- Cointribune, "AI Agents: Beijing Sets the Rules, China's Tech Giants Fall in Line" — https://www.cointribune.com/en/ai-agents-beijing-sets-the-rules-chinas-tech-giants-fall-in-line

Claims sourced here (as reported): China's Cyberspace Administration (CAC), together with the NDRC and MIIT, issued the Implementation Opinions on the Standardized Application and Innovative Development of Intelligent Agents, effective 15 July 2026 — described as the world's first dedicated regulatory category for AI agents. The Opinions define AI agents as intelligent systems capable of autonomous perception, memory, decision-making, interaction and execution, and establish a three-tier decision-authorization structure that classifies agent actions by consequence level and requires human-approval thresholds scaled accordingly. Organizations deploying agents in high-risk / sensitive sectors — including healthcare, transportation, media and public safety — must complete formal filing with regulators and face compliance testing and product-recall provisions. Organizations operating agents in Chinese markets without a formal decision-authorization policy are treated as non-compliant as of 15 July 2026.

- Hunton Andrews Kurth, "Illinois Governor Signs Frontier AI Model Law" — https://www.hunton.com/privacy-and-cybersecurity-law-blog/illinois-governor-signs-frontier-ai-model-law
- McDonald Hopkins, "Big tech, meet big oversight: An overview of Illinois' Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act" — https://www.mcdonaldhopkins.com/insights/news/illinois-artificial-intelligence-safety-measures-act

Claims sourced here: On 6 July 2026 Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 315, the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act, making Illinois the first US state to require annual independent third-party audits of frontier AI models. The Act creates obligations for "large frontier developers" — frontier developers with annual gross revenues exceeding $500 million — including publishing how their products could pose a "catastrophic risk" and how such risks would be addressed, retaining an independent third party each year for a compliance audit, and reporting "critical safety incidents" to the state within 72 hours. The requirements take effect 1 January 2028. Anthropic supported the bill, stating it "takes the safety practices leading labs already follow voluntarily — publishing a safety framework, transparent reporting, protecting whistleblowers — and helps establish a baseline that every leading AI developer is expected to meet." Coverage notes that, with federal AI legislation unlikely in the near term, state laws are effectively creating a national compliance standard.

- beam.ai, "EU AI Act 2026: GPAI Enforcement & 3% Fines Begin" — https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/eu-ai-act-enforcement-august-2-2026-gpai-fines

Claims sourced here: From 2 August 2026 the European Commission's enforcement powers over general-purpose AI (GPAI) providers become fully applicable — including the power to request documentation and information (Article 91), conduct evaluations (Article 92), request measures including risk mitigation and market restriction (Article 93), and impose fines of up to €15 million or 3% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher (Article 101). The date does not create new GPAI obligations; it makes existing ones enforceable. Models placed on the market on or after 2 August 2025 must comply with no grace period; obligations flow down to deployers.
